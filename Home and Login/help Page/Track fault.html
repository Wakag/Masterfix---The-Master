Maintenance faults statistics
1. Understanding the Pie Chart
• The pie chart provides a visual representation of the current state of maintenance tickets.
• Each segment is color-coded to represent a specific status or category of faults:
    • Requisition: Represents maintenance issues that have been requested.
    • Open: Faults that are currently in progress.
    • Confirmed: Faults that have been confirmed for action.
    • Resolved: Faults that have been fixed but not yet closed.
    • Closed: Maintenance faults that are fully resolved and completed.

• Hover over the pie chart segments to see detailed stats for each category, including the number of faults and the percentage of the total.
• This chart helps you to quickly understand the progress and status of maintenance requests for your residence.
2. Understanding the Card Format
• The card format is used to display essential statistics on maintenance faults.
• There are two main types of cards:
    • Faults by Residence: Displays the total number of faults for each residence for a specific semester.
    • Turnaround Time Stats: Shows how long it took to resolve tickets.

• Faults by Residence: Each card represents a residence (e.g., "Chris Hani House") and displays the total faults for that residence for the selected semester.
    • Use these cards to compare fault reports between different residences.

• Turnaround Time: These cards show how long it took to resolve tickets:
    • More than 7 days: Faults that took over a week to resolve.
    • 4-7 days: Tickets resolved within this time period.
    • Less than 1 day: Urgent tickets resolved in under a day.
